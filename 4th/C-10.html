WHEREAS veterans in their line of duty have shown extreme acts of bravery and courage;
AND WHEREAS veterans have neglected needs when returning to civilian life,

NOW, THEREFORE, Her Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the House of Commons of Canada, enacts as follows:

Short Title

Short Title

  1. This Act may be cited as the Support for Veterans Act.

Interpretation

Definitions

  1. In this Act,
“veteran”
means a person who has served in the Canadian Forces, either in combat or non-combat roles;
“domicile”
means an official registered residence of a citizen, specifically, but not limited to, a constructed home or empty plot of land;
“combat role”
is a role assigned to an individual who is faced with a task which is perceived to involve a confrontational nature;
“non-combat role”
is a role assigned to an individual who is faced with a task which is not perceived to involve a confrontational nature, but still may face danger due to their location and other factors.

Programs

Budget increase

  1. The funding to the Department of Veterans Affairs is increased by $750,000 minimum, to a maximum of $2,000,000 to fund the programs established by this Act, from the Direct Transfer to Departments fund stipulated by the Budget of Canada 2016.

Construction of facilities

  1. The Department of Veterans Affairs may create and expedite the construction of necessary facilities relating to medical care of the veterans and other branches of the Department.

Access to facilities

  1. Any veteran must have complete and full access without charge to the facilities created by the Department of Veterans Affairs, noting medical and mental facilities.

Education grant

  1. (1) Any person returning from a combat or non-combat role, unless dishonourably discharged or for other reasons, must, if requested, be allowed education grants and funding paid by the Department of Veterans Affairs, provided that the veteran has applied with the procedure prescribed by the Minister of Veterans Affairs and has been accepted into an institution which has been recognized as a lawful educational institution providing secondary education by the state.
    (2) Any supplies such as books required to facilitate the education of a veteran sponsored by the Department of Veterans Affairs must be paid in full by the Department of Veterans Affairs at standard market price.

Housing loan

  1. (1) Any person returning from a combat or non-combat role, unless dishonourably discharged or for other reasons, must, if requested, be allowed to apply for a loan without monthly interest collection specifically for the purpose of purchasing a domicile.
    (2) A loan provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs for the purpose of the purchasing of a domicile must not exceed $500,000 and is to be collected on a monthly basis with no interest rate, paid in total over 25 years.
    (3) A loan provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs for the purpose of the purchasing of a domicile must have the maximum adjusted every year to reflect changes in housing prices and inflation rates.

Adjustment funding

  1. Veterans who require readjustment when returning back from service are to be provided with a weekly stipend equal to the weekly cost of living in their respective provinces, paid by the Department of Veterans Affairs for a maximum of 16 weeks.

Employment facilitation

  1. The Department of Veterans Affairs must provide facilities to aid and encourage employment of veterans when veterans return to a civilian role and are unemployed.

Temporary housing

  1. The Department of Veterans Affairs medical facilities may be used, if necessary, to act as temporary housing for veterans if they are not able to sustain a healthy standard of living as determined by the Department of Veterans Affairs.

Coming into Force

Coming into force

  1. This Act will come into force three months after it receives royal assent.
